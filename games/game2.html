Murder at Midnight

MISSION BRIEFING: A prominent businessman, Richard Sterling, was found dead in his mansion during a dinner party at exactly midnight. Five guests were present at the time of the murder. Examine the crime scene carefully, collect evidence, and identify the killer. Time is of the essence, detective.

Evidence #1: Bloodstained Knife

The murder weapon appears to be a kitchen knife from the victim's own collection. Fingerprints were wiped clean, suggesting premeditation. The pattern of blood spatter indicates a right-handed attacker.

Evidence #2: Victim's Phone

The victim's phone shows a text message sent at 11:45 PM: "We need to talk about the documents. Meet me in the study at midnight." The sender's number is unlisted.

Evidence #3: Wine Glass

A wine glass with traces of lipstick was found on the desk in the study. Lab analysis confirms it matches the shade worn by only one of the female guests.

Evidence #4: Crumpled Note

A crumpled note found in the fireplace, partially burned. The visible text reads: "...will expose your fraud if you don't pay by tomorrow. Your partner..."

Evidence #5: Broken Watch

The victim's watch is broken, stopped at exactly 12:07 AM. This likely indicates the precise time of death.
